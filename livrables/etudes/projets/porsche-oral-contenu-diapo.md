## PARTIE 1 — Strengths: R&D & Manufacturing

### Slide 1 — INTRO
**Titre :** 1. Strengths — R&D & Manufacturing
**Contenu :** Porsche's engineering backbone: world-class R&D and premium manufacturing.

### Slide 2 — R&D Capabilities
**Titre :** R&D Capabilities
**Contenu :**
- €4–5 billion invested in R&D every year
- Weissach center: one of the most advanced in the industry
- Key tech: electric motors, hybrids (918 Spyder), aerodynamics, PDK gearbox
- VW Group / Porsche SE → pooled R&D resources
- Heavy bet on electrification → Taycan as a benchmark

### Slide 3 — Manufacturing Facilities
**Titre :** Manufacturing Facilities
**Contenu :**
- Zuffenhausen (Stuttgart): builds 911 & Taycan, artisanal precision
- Leipzig: builds Macan & Cayenne, environmentally certified
- Flexible, premium production: each car almost bespoke
- Capacity ≈ 300,000 vehicles / year

## PARTIE 2 — Internal Strengths (SWOT)

### Slide 4 — INTRO
**Titre :** 2. Internal Strengths — SWOT Analysis
**Contenu :** The internal assets that set Porsche apart from every rival.

### Slide 5 — Strength 1
**Titre :** 1. Exceptional Brand Value & Reputation
**Contenu :**
- Interbrand: brand valued at $13.5bn, 10th automotive brand worldwide
- Brand Finance: #1 luxury brand in the world, $33.7bn
- Ahead of Louis Vuitton and Gucci, 2nd year running
- Remarkable for a brand that does not mass-produce

### Slide 6 — Strength 2
**Titre :** 2. Superior Customer Loyalty
**Contenu :**
- J.D. Power: #1 in loyalty among premium makers, 57.4%
- Higher than Ferrari, Lamborghini or Mercedes
- Built on a strong emotional connection with customers

### Slide 7 — Strength 3
**Titre :** 3. Profit Margins Well Above Rivals
**Contenu :**
- Most profitable brand in VW Group: 12% of revenue but 29% of profits (2022)
- Same profit on 314k cars as Audi on 902k
- Operating margin above 18%
- Avg profit €20,450/car vs €7,100 Audi, €1,020 VW

### Slide 8 — Strength 4
**Titre :** 4. Motorsport Heritage & Driving Experience
**Contenu :**
- 19 wins at the 24 Hours of Le Mans, ahead of Ferrari & McLaren
- More than marketing: engineering transferred to road cars
- An edge pure-luxury rivals (Bentley, Maserati) can't match

### Slide 9 — Strength 5
**Titre :** 5. Product Differentiation & Innovation
**Contenu :**
- Distinctive features that stand out and attract buyers
- Unique span: sports car (911), premium SUV (Cayenne, Macan), serious EV (Taycan)
- Ferrari stays confined to sports cars
- Brand = prestige & status, drives high-end sales

### Slide 10 — Strength 6
**Titre :** 6. Volkswagen Group Synergies
**Contenu :**
- Strategic alliances within the industry
- Shared platforms (J1 with Audi for Taycan / e-tron GT)
- Economies of scale on components + global R&D network
- A lever Ferrari and Aston Martin lack

## PARTIE 4 — Internal Weaknesses (SWOT)

### Slide 16 — INTRO
**Titre :** 4. Internal Weaknesses — SWOT Analysis
**Contenu :** Internal negative factors that limit Porsche's value and flexibility.

### Slide 17 — Weaknesses (toutes les questions) — SLIDE CHARGÉE
**Titre :** Internal Weaknesses
**Contenu :**
- Prohibitive pricing & niche positioning → excludes middle class, vulnerable to recessions
- High maintenance costs & long delivery times → buyers turn to Lexus / Tesla
- EV transition & China dependence → weak China demand cuts 2025 volumes
- Not fully independent → €5bn impairment (2024) tied to VW; rising raw-material costs
- Production concentrated in Stuttgart → high labor/energy costs + logistical fragility

## PARTIE 5 — External Opportunities (SWOT × PESTLE)

### Slide 18 — INTRO
**Titre :** 5. External Opportunities — SWOT × PESTLE
**Contenu :** External trends Porsche can ride to keep growing.

### Slide 19 — Opportunities (les questions) — SLIDE CHARGÉE
**Titre :** External Opportunities
**Contenu :**
- Booming luxury EV market → $463bn by 2030, $1.04tn by 2034; 62.5% of luxury buyers prioritize sustainability
- Strong brand momentum → H1 2025: 36.1% electrified deliveries (+14.5 pts), share up in 5 of 6 lines
- Geographic shift → focus North America (+5%) & emerging markets vs Germany (-23%), China (-28%); capture disappointed Tesla buyers
- Software-defined vehicles → new recurring revenue; critical window 2026–2028

## PARTIE 6 — PESTEL Analysis (Threats Facing Porsche)

### Slide 20 — INTRO
**Titre :** 6. PESTEL Analysis — Threats Facing Porsche
**Contenu :** The macro forces threatening Porsche, factor by factor.

### Slide 21 — P (Political)
**Titre :** P — Political
**Contenu :**
- Rising tariffs & trade tensions: heavy exporter, no US plant
- Sensitive Europe–China relations threaten access to a key market

### Slide 22 — E (Economic)
**Titre :** E — Economic
**Contenu :**
- Sharp sales decline in China (slowdown + local rivals)
- Luxury market slowdown, demand tied to global uncertainty
- EV transition & investments pressure profitability

### Slide 23 — S (Sociocultural)
**Titre :** S — Sociocultural
**Contenu :**
- Younger buyers value tech, connectivity, sustainability
- Risk of fading prestige in some markets (esp. China, new local premium brands)

### Slide 24 — T (Technological)
**Titre :** T — Technological
**Contenu :**
- Rising EV competition: BYD, Xiaomi with advanced software & batteries
- Slower-than-expected EV adoption forces strategy shifts

### Slide 25 — E (Environmental)
**Titre :** E — Environmental
**Contenu :**
- Decarbonization pressure → massive clean-tech investment
- Supply-chain scrutiny on raw materials & battery production

### Slide 26 — L (Legal)
**Titre :** L — Legal
**Contenu :**
- Stricter EU rules: cybersecurity, software compliance, safety
- Tightening CO₂ emissions rules → penalties & strategic shifts

## PARTIE 7 — Micro-Environment Analysis

### Slide 27 — INTRO
**Titre :** 7. Micro-Environment Analysis
**Contenu :** The actors closest to Porsche that shape its ability to serve customers.

### Slide 28 — The Company
**Titre :** The Company (Porsche AG)
**Contenu :**
- Revenue €40.1bn (2024); SUVs = 47% of revenue
- HQ Stuttgart, CEO Oliver Blume, ~41,000 employees
- VW Group subsidiary with strategic & brand autonomy
- Six model lines: 911, 718, Taycan, Panamera, Macan, Cayenne

### Slide 29 — Suppliers
**Titre :** Suppliers
**Contenu :**
- Mostly German Tier 1: Bosch, Continental, ZF, Schaeffler, HELLA
- Supply powertrain electronics, ADAS, transmissions, brakes, tires
- European software alliance: foundation by 2026, cars by 2030
- Risk: mass layoffs (ZF 11–14k, Continental 7,150) weaken reliability

### Slide 30 — Intermediaries — SLIDE CHARGÉE
**Titre :** Intermediaries
**Contenu :**
- ~840 Porsche Centers worldwide: sales, financing, after-sales
- Porsche Financial Services: leasing, financing, insurance
- Logistics partners: Stuttgart & Leipzig to 120+ countries
- Digital intermediaries: Porsche App Center (Alexa, navigation)
- 2024–25 China failures showed how critical this layer is

### Slide 31 — Customers — SLIDE CHARGÉE
**Titre :** Customers
**Contenu :**
- Avg household income > $500k, mostly male (women 18.5% in 2024)
- Avg age ~50, but Taycan attracts under-45 / new-to-brand buyers
- High-income professionals, executives, entrepreneurs
- Wealthy hubs: LA, London, Shanghai; rising Middle East & SE Asia
- B2B fleet segment: reliability, total cost of ownership, sustainability

### Slide 32 — Competitors — SLIDE CHARGÉE
**Titre :** Competitors
**Contenu :**
- Sports cars: Ferrari, Lamborghini, Aston Martin, McLaren
- Luxury SUV/sedan: Mercedes AMG, BMW M, Audi RS / Bentley, Range Rover
- EV (most disruptive): Tesla, Lucid, Chinese premium (Xiaomi SU7, NIO, BYD)

### Slide 33 — Publics — SLIDE CHARGÉE
**Titre :** Publics
**Contenu :**
- Financial: Porsche SE (53.3% voting rights), investors, analysts
- Media: auto press, Bloomberg/FT, YouTubers
- Government: EU, German govt, Chinese authorities, US NHTSA
- Citizen-action: environmental NGOs, consumer groups
- Internal: 41,000+ employees, works council (Betriebsrat)
- Local: communities around Stuttgart & Leipzig plants
